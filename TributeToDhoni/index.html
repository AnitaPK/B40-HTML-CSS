Achievements

Year Discription
Winning the ICC Cricket World Cup 2011 (ODI) Dhoni led India to its second World Cup victory after 28 years, defeating Sri Lanka in the final, with his iconic unbeaten 91-run knock.
Winning the ICC T20 World Cup 2007 Under Dhoni's captaincy, India won the inaugural T20 World Cup in South Africa, establishing him as a leading captain on the global stage.
Winning the ICC Champions Trophy 2013 Dhoni became the first captain to win all three ICC limited-overs trophies (World Cup, T20 World Cup, and Champions Trophy).
Winning the IPL (Indian Premier League) Titles (2010, 2011, 2018, 2021, and 2023) the captain of Chennai Super Kings (CSK), Dhoni has led his team to five IPL titles, making CSK one of the most successful franchises in the tournament's history.
Winning the ICC Test Mace (2009, 2010) Under his leadership, India became the No. 1 ranked Test team for the first time and held the position for 18 months.
Winning the Asia Cup (2010 and 2016) Dhoni led India to two Asia Cup victories, one in the ODI format (2010) and another in the T20 format (2016).
Winning the Champions League T20 Titles (2010, 2014) Dhoni's CSK won two Champions League T20 titles, showcasing their dominance in T20 leagues globally.
Most Successful Indian ODI Captain Dhoni is the most successful Indian ODI captain with 110 wins in 200 matches.
Only Captain to Win All Three Major ICC Trophies (World Cup, T20 World Cup, Champions Trophy) He is the only captain to have achieved this feat in the history of cricket.
Most Stumpings in International Cricket Dhoni holds the record for the most stumpings in international cricket, with over 195 stumpings across all formats.
Most Matches as Captain in International Cricket (332 matches) Dhoni has captained India in more matches than any other captain in history.
Scoring 10,000 Runs in ODI Cricket Dhoni is one of the few players to have crossed 10,000 runs in ODIs, and he achieved this milestone with one of the highest averages among wicketkeeper-batsmen.
ICC ODI Player of the Year (2008, 2009) Dhoni won this prestigious award for two consecutive years, becoming the first player to do so.
Fastest Century by an Indian Wicketkeeper (148 runs vs Pakistan in 2005) This innings remains one of the most memorable and established Dhoni as a force in international cricket.
Lieutenant Colonel Honorary Rank in Indian Territorial Army (2011) Dhoni was awarded this honorary rank, showing recognition of his contribution to Indian sports and patriotism.
